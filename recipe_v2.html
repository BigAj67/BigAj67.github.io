Deviled eggs

History of the eggs

The earliest record of a “deviled”egg (a stuffed egg) was discovered in the 13th century in Andalucia, Ancient Rome. This would be Southern Spain today. While I don't have that recipe to show you, I did find another Italian one from the 13th century! Oddly exciting. This has been translated by the lovely Sannie B of A Dollop of History. According to her research and adaptation of the recipe, Liber de Coquina, people enjoyed a fried spiced stuffed egg.

recipe

  • 6 large eggs
  • 3 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon mustard (yellow or Dijon)
  • 1 teaspoon white vinegar or lemon juice
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • Paprika, for garnish
  • Optional mix-ins: pickle relish, minced chives, hot sauce, garlic powder

How to make them

  1. Boil the eggs
    1. Place the eggs in a single layer in a pot.
    2. Cover with water so there's about 1 inch above the eggs.
    3. Bring to a full boil over medium-high heat.
    4. When boiling, turn off the heat, cover, and let sit for 10-12 minutes.
  2. Cool and peel
    1. Transfer eggs to an ice bath (or run under very cold water)
      for at least 5 minutes.
    2. Gently crack and peel the shells.
  3. Prepare the filling
    1. Slice eggs in half lengthwise.
    2. Remove the yolks and place them in a bowl.
    3. Mash yolks with a fork until smooth.
    4. Add mayonnaise, mustard, vinegar, salt, and pepper.
    5. Mix until creamy (add more mayo if you want them softer).
  4. Fill the eggs
    • Spoon the yolk mixture back into the egg whites or pipe it in with a piping bag or zip-top bag.
  5. Garnish and serve
    1. Sprinkle with paprika.
    2. Optionally add chives, bacon bits, or pickles.
