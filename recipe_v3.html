Pancakes

A pancake is a versatile, flat cake prepared from a starch-based batter and cooked on a hot surface like a griddle or frying pan. The batter typically consists of staple ingredients such as flour, eggs, milk, and butter. Depending on the recipe and cultural variation, the resulting dish can be thin and delicate, like a French crêpe, or thick and fluffy, like an American buttermilk pancake which uses baking powder as a leavening agent to create an airy interior.

  • ingredients
  • eggs
  • flour
  • sugar
  • salt
  • baking powder/soda
  • intruction
  • Combine dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Set this aside.
  • Mix wet ingredients: In a separate, medium bowl, whisk together the milk, egg, and melted butter (or oil).
  • Combine wet and dry: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry mixture. Stir until just combined. It is important not to overmix; a few lumps are okay, as overmixing can make the pancakes tough.
  • Heat the pan: Place a griddle or nonstick frying pan over medium heat and brush lightly with butter or oil.
  • Pour the batter: Once the pan is hot, use approximately 1/4 cup of batter for each pancake, pouring it onto the griddle.
  • Cook and flip: Cook for 1 to 2 minutes, or until bubbles form on the surface and the edges look dry and set. Flip the pancake over with a spatula and cook for another 1 to 2 minutes on the other side, or until golden brown and cooked through.
  • Serve: Remove the finished pancakes from the pan. You can keep them warm in a low oven while you cook the rest of the batter. Serve hot with your favorite toppings, such as maple syrup, butter, or fresh fruit.

Chocolate chip Pancake

A chocolate chip pancake is a golden-brown, soft, and fluffy griddlecake studded with an abundance of melty, gooey chocolate chips. It combines the classic, light texture and savory flavor of a traditional pancake with bursts of sweet chocolate in every bite.

fruit Pancake

A fruit pancake is a versatile dish featuring a pancake, either thin like a French crêpe or thick and fluffy like an American pancake, combined with the natural sweetness and texture of various fruits. The fruit can be incorporated into the batter during cooking or added as a topping afterwards, and the final presentation is often colorful and satisfying.
